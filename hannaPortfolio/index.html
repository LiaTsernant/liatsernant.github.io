About

Violinist Anna Gribkova started violin studies at the age of 5 at Minsk Academy in her native Belarus.

She continued her studies at the Special School of Music affiliated with the St. Petersburg Conservatory in the class of Prof. Savely Shalman.
Following her concert debut in Italy in 2009, Ms. Gribkova has performed in recitals and as a soloist with orchestra at numerous venues in Belarus, Russia, Europe and the United States. In 2014 Anna had been awarded a scholarship from the Glazunov foundation, and in 2015 she had won a scholarship to participate at the summer academy in Murren, Switzerland and was also admitted at the Rimsky-Korsakov Conservatory of Music in St. Petersburg.

Currently, Ms. Gribkova is a Woodruff scholar at the Schwob School of Music studying with Prof. Sergiu Schwartz, the William B. and Sue Marie Turner Distinguished Chair in Violin. Anna performed with the Schwob Philharmonic Orchestra as a winner of 2017-18 CSU/Schwob Conservatory of music Concerto Competition at River Center of the Performing Arts. She has also appeared as a winner of Atlanta Music Club competition and Samuel Fordis Young Artist Concerto competition.
This year she will appear with the Schwob Philharmonic as a winner of the 2019-20 Concerto competition.
